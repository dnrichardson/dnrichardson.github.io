About Dale

Current status

I’m a postdoc in the Plant Molecular Biology Laboratory at the Instituto Gulbenkian de Ciência (IGC), a research institution located just outside of Lisbon, Portugal. I primarily rely on R and a suite of bioinformatics tools to analyze large-scale genomic changes in response to abiotic stress in the model plant organism, Arabidopsis thaliana, commonly known as the “thale cress”.

For the past three years, I have been working primarily to understand how a particular gene is involved in regulating the seed germination process when seeds are grown in a high-salt environment. As high salinity soils are major problem for plant growth worldwide, this research may help understand important aspects of gene-level adaptation to salt stress and perhaps even faciliate tolerance to salt stress in agronomically important plants.
